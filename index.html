AGRICULTURAL COMMODITIES IN AUSTRALIA

Farming plays a crucial role in Australia's economy and culture. Agriculture alone contributes approximately 3% to Australia's GDP and employs around 230,000 people. Australian agriculture is broadly divided into two main categories: Livestock and Crops. Due to land, climate and conditions varying throughout the country, many states produce different types of commodities with diverse production levels. Commodities refer to the raw materials and products, for example Wheat and Rice for Crops, and Milk and Wool for Livestock.

Agricultural Productivity Across Australia

Value ($m) per Square Kilometre

The map above highlights the total value of commodities (in million dollars) per km squared of land area. Since many territories and states vary in size, this metric provides a more equitable comparison of agricultural productivity across regions. It accounts for the fact that larger states might have higher total output simply due to their greater land area, while smaller states or territories could be more efficient in their use of available land. This approach allows for a clearer understanding of which areas are maximising their agricultural potential relative to their size, rather than just looking at absolute production values.
